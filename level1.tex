\subsection{Uncertainty in Geophysical Doppler Shift (DCA method)}

\subsubsection{Step 1: Definition of measurand and measurement model}

In the \ac{DCA} method \cite{chapron05}, the geophysical Doppler frequency
shift, i.e., the measurand, is given by
\begin{equation}
  \varpi_g = \varpi_{\rm dc} - \varpi_p + \Delta\varpi_g
  \label{eq:DCcomponents}
\end{equation}

\noindent where $\varpi_{\rm dc}$ is the Doppler centroid frequency shift,
$\varpi_p$ is the Doppler shift predicted from an estimate of the antenna
pointing, and $\Delta\varpi_g$ is the residual uncertainty. The
mathematical model for the predicted Doppler shift is $\varpi_p = \varpi_{\rm
geo} + \varpi_{\rm e} + \Delta\varpi_p$, where $\varpi_{\rm geo}$ is a
geometric term related to the antenna pointing via the satellite's orbit and
attitude, $\varpi_{\rm e}$ is an electronic term resulting from antenna
mis-pointing, and $\Delta\varpi_p$ is the prediction uncertainty. The estimate
of each term comes with uncertainty as described in the following sub-sections.

\noindent The Maturity of Uncertainty estimate given in
Table~\ref{tab:doppler_effects_table} is defined as
\begin{enumerate}
    \setcounter{enumi}{-1}
  \item Effect identified, no quantification;
  \item Estimates only;
  \item Some analysis performed to evaluate; and
  \item Rigorous analysis performed.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent Likewise, the Maturity of Correlation Scale estimate given in
Table~\ref{tab:doppler_effects_table} is defined as
\begin{enumerate}
    \setcounter{enumi}{-1}
  \item Not done;
  \item Estimated;
  \item Scale based on analysis, unsure about correlation shape; and
  \item Strong evidence for correlation scale and shape.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Step 4: Data Product and Uncertainties}

% USE PDF PLOTS
Once the geophysical Doppler shift, $\varpi_{\rm g}$, i.e., the measurand, has
been calculated, its uncertainty can be calculated by considering its variance
over land areas with uniform backscatter, such as the Amazon region. Since the
complete analysis is performed by using land cover data, there is no need to do
a complete error tracing. Figure~\ref{fig:PDFs} shows the \ac{PDF} of each of
the five subswaths in the \ac{WS} mode Envisat \ac{ASAR} data.

As shown, the uncertainty is between 4.7 and 6.2 Hz depending on the subswath.
The larger uncertainties of subswaths 1, 3, and 5 are expected from increased
aliasing due to the low azimuth sampling rate (about 1700 Hz) in those
subswaths, compared to subswaths 2 and 4 (about 2100 Hz) \cite{hansen26}.

\subsubsection{Step 5: Documentation}

The uncertainty of the geophysical Doppler shift retrievals are provided in
\cite{hansen26}, and can be calculated from openly available datasets at
\url{https://data.met.no} by analysing land cover data. For further
documentation, we refer to the literature, e.g.,
\cite{chapron05,MoiseevJGRO2020} and references therein.
